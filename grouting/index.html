Grouting Design in Kamloops: Precision Injection for Ground Improvement

Practical geotechnics, field-tested.

LEARN MORE

A contractor on the North Shore ran into a problem at 18 feet. Glacial till with cobbles. Groundwater pouring in. Standard dewatering failed. The fix was a targeted permeation grouting program. We designed the injection grid, the mix rheology, and the real-time QA/QC protocol. Kamloops has a geology that punishes generic solutions. The valley floor is a complex sequence of glaciofluvial deposits, lacustrine silts, and coarse alluvial fans fed by the Thompson rivers. A grouting design here has to account for particle size distribution, hydraulic conductivity, and the aggressive groundwater chemistry. Get one parameter wrong and the grout goes where you don't want it. Our approach ties the injection plan to data from in-situ permeability testing, because you can't design a grout curtain on assumptions.

A grouting program without a verified particle-size-dependent rheology is just pumping money into the ground.

How we work

The National Building Code of Canada and CSA A23.3 set the structural performance benchmarks, but grouting design lives in the geotechnical realm where ASTM D4320 for chemical grouts and ASTM C942 for cementitious mixes apply. In Kamloops, the seasonal fluctuation of the water table along the Thompson River valley adds another layer of complexity. A design that works in August can fail in April when hydrostatic pressure peaks. We specify grout types based on the fines content of the target stratum. For the silty units common in lower Sahali, microfine cement or sodium silicate grouts are necessary. For the open-work gravels near the airport, a stable cement-bentonite mix achieves the required penetration. The injection sequence matters too. We sequence primary, secondary, and tertiary holes to build an impermeable barrier progressively, verifying each stage with water pressure tests.

Site-specific factors

Glacial Lake Thompson left behind thick sequences of varved silts and clays. These deposits are prone to internal erosion when subjected to high hydraulic gradients. A dam cutoff or deep excavation in these soils without a properly designed grout curtain risks piping failure. The 2017 Elephant Hill wildfire stripped vegetation from slopes west of Kamloops. Post-fire debris flows have changed the grain size distribution in several creeks, increasing the fine fraction. This alters the injectability of the ground. A design based on pre-fire soil data may underestimate the required grout volume by 30% or more. The other risk is hydrofracturing. In the stiff, overconsolidated till north of the city, excessive injection pressure can open fractures and create preferential flow paths, bypassing the target zone entirely.

Frequently asked questions

What does grouting design cost for a typical Kamloops project?

Design fees for a grouting program typically range from CA$1,590 for a small, single-line curtain to CA$6,220 for a complex project like a dam cutoff or deep excavation requiring multiple grout types and staged injection. The final cost depends on the number of boreholes, the depth of the treatment zone, and the level of QA/QC specified.

How do you select the right grout for Kamloops soils?

Grout selection is dictated by the grain size distribution of the target soil. We use the soil's D15 and coefficient of permeability to determine injectability. For the fine silts in the city's lower benches, microfine cements with a maximum particle size under 10 microns are required. For the clean gravels near the floodplain, ordinary Portland cement with bentonite is effective and economical.

How long does a grouting design take to prepare?

A complete grouting design package, including mix trials, injection sequence, and instrumentation layout, typically takes two to three weeks. This assumes the geotechnical site investigation data, including grain size analysis and field permeability tests, is already available.

Can grouting stabilize the collapsible soils found in some Kamloops areas?

Yes. Compaction grouting is effective for collapsible alluvial fan deposits. A low-mobility grout is injected under high pressure to displace and densify the soil matrix. The design must specify the grout consistency and injection rate to achieve compaction without hydrofracturing the overlying crust.
